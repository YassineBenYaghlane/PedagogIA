**Approche spiralaire** = a pedagogical orientation in which the same savoir, savoir-faire, or compétence is **revisited across years**, each time strengthened, refined, or extended. Explicitly prescribed by the FWB math référentiel ([[sources/referentiel-mathematiques]]) as a guiding principle alongside annualization.

## Canonical phrasing from the référentiel

> *"Un même savoir-faire ou une même compétence peut être formulé(e) à l'identique d'une année à l'autre mais en précisant alors une gradation relative au niveau d'autonomie attendu (« avec » ou « sans l'aide de l'enseignant », par exemple)."*

Concretely: the attendu may repeat verbatim across years, with difficulty progressing via autonomy level, number range, context richness, or problem complexity.

## Examples in primary math

- **Multiplication tables** — introduced T2/T5/T10 in P2, adds T3/T4/T6 in P3, adds T7/T8/T9 in P4, consolidated in P5/P6. Same skill, widening scope. See [[concepts/tables-multiplication]].
- **Number range** — *"compter, dire, lire, écrire"* is revisited every year from ≤20 (P1) to milliards (P6). See [[concepts/numeration-decimale]].
- **Problem solving** — *"résoudre un problème en mobilisant des opérations"* appears every year P1–P6, but gradually adding operations (+, −, then ×, then ÷), larger numbers, decimals, and multi-step démarches. See [[concepts/resolution-problemes-math]].

## Why this matters for PedagogIA

- **Validates spaced repetition.** The curriculum itself is spiral — revisiting what was learned is expected, not remedial.
- **Justifies our mastery-decay model.** Even "mastered" skills must re-surface.
- **Implies multi-year skill edges in the DAG.** A single conceptual skill can have several attendu "layers" corresponding to different years, with prerequisites across the spiral.
