Three kinds of évaluation are named by the FWB's Code de l'enseignement and re-cited in [[sources/referentiel-mathematiques]] §3.1:

- **Formative** — during learning, aimed at helping the student progress by diagnosing difficulties; can support self-evaluation.
- **Sommative** — a bilan at the end of a sequence / year.
- **Certificative** — external, delivers a certificate (e.g. CEB at end of primary).

The référentiel is unusually assertive about the **primacy of formative evaluation**:

> *"L'élève n'apprend pas pour être évalué, ses acquis sont évalués pour l'aider à apprendre."*

## Key operational rules

- Mastery of an attendu can be established *without* a formal évaluation — reactivation in later activities counts.
- Non-maîtrise should trigger **personalized support** (stratégies d'accompagnement, remédiation immédiate), not automatic redoublement.
- The [[entities/pacte-enseignement-excellence]] targets **−50 % redoublement by 2030**; grade retention is explicitly positioned as a last resort, after personalized support has failed.

## Why this matters for PedagogIA

- The app's entire orientation — diagnostic → targeted drill → investigation on error → spaced practice — is textbook formative evaluation. The curriculum *endorses this approach in writing*, which is unusually strong alignment for an edtech product vs a national curriculum.
- Scoring UX should reflect this: grading is diagnostic feedback, not judgement. (Already embedded in the JARDIN design principle "*rose soft error, never bright red*".)
- Reporting to parents should frame metrics as *progress signals*, not grades — same framing the référentiel uses for teachers.
