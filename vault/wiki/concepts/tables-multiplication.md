The multiplication tables from T2 to T10 — one of the explicit **automatismes de base** the FWB math référentiel requires to be memorized by end of primary. Introduction is staged across P2–P4.

## Introduction schedule

| Year | New tables | Cumulative known |
|---|---|---|
| **P2** | T2, T5, T10 | T2, T5, T10 |
| **P3** | T3, T4, T6 | T2, T3, T4, T5, T6, T10 |
| **P4** | T7, T8, T9 | T2 → T10 (complete) |
| **P5** | — | consolidation + décompositions de 100 en deux facteurs |
| **P6** | — | maîtrise |

## What the attendus expect beyond rote

The référentiel doesn't just ask for recall — it explicitly wants students to:

1. **Construct** tables from situations, drawings, and additions réitérées (*"à partir de situations / avec des dessins / en mots / en calculs"*).
2. **Express regularities** within a table (e.g. "the T5 always ends in 0 or 5") and **between tables** (T2 and T4 linked, T3 and T6 linked, T5 and T10 linked).
3. **Know by heart** ("connaitre de mémoire"), eventually including selective divisions (P3: ÷10, ÷4; P4: +÷10, ÷100, ÷5, ÷4, ÷8; P5: ÷50, ÷25).

Rote recall without understanding of regularities is an incomplete answer to the attendu.

## Why this matters for PedagogIA

- A **drill-heavy** area of the app that aligns cleanly with retrieval practice and spaced repetition. Natural sandbox for the streak / XP loop.
- **Family exercises** (pattern completion, regularity spotting) are as much expected as straight flashcards.
- Hint strategies can lean on **décomposition tricks**: T4 via T2+T2, T6 via T3+T3, T8 via T4+T4, T9 via T10−T1… These are exactly the *"procédures de calcul mental"* the curriculum also expects (see [[concepts/champ-3-arithmetique]] mental-calc row).
