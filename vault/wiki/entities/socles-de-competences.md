The prior FWB curriculum, which prevailed for roughly 20 years in *enseignement fondamental* and the early *enseignement secondaire*. Being replaced progressively from 2022 to 2028 by the new tronc-commun référentiels issued under the [[entities/pacte-enseignement-excellence]].

## Why it's being replaced

Per the [[sources/referentiel-mathematiques]] avant-propos: society and the world "changed considerably" since the Socles were written; expectations of school needed to evolve — both to hit the Pacte's objectives and to prepare students for 21st-century societal demands. The new référentiels re-evaluate, clarify, and increase precision of progressions (see [[concepts/contenu-vs-attendu]]).

## Continuity, not rupture

The référentiels explicitly note that "*une certaine continuité avec l'ancien n'a pas été exclue, loin de là : des pans importants de la formation commune antérieure conservent leur pertinence*". So: not a tabula rasa — a renaming and re-tuning of a large fraction of the Socles content, plus new material.

## Relevance to PedagogIA

- Teachers trained in the Socles era may still frame their classroom practice in the old vocabulary. Surface any mismatch when it shows up in user feedback.
- Legacy textbooks, exam papers, and teacher-forum advice often cite Socles competencies. Treat with caution — our skill tree should track the new référentiel, not Socles.
