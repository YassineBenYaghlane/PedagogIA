The **2015 CEB** ([[entities/ceb]]). Math arithmetic booklet = **Livret 7 Nombres et opérations, /30 pts, Jeudi 18 juin 2015, ~16 questions** (PDF pages 94–118 of `raw/CEB_2015_Questionnaires.pdf`). Significantly lower point budget than 2017+ (/70) but denser questions.

## Full question inventory

- **Q1 — 10-op compute block.** `48×5, 700×70, 128+587+113+172, 4×250×0×20 (zero-absorption trap), 100 000−587, 998+587, 366÷6, 0,72÷1 (identity-trick), 7 603−399, 150% de 600`. Confirms `pure_compute_timed_block` and percentages >100% as Champ-3 staples since at least 2015.
- **Q2 — NEW: quick estimate with format constraint.** *Estime ce que coutera cet achat. Écris un nombre à 3 chiffres qui se termine par `0`.* Five prices to total (`50,50 + 28,99 + 19,40 + 148,50 + 87,10`) ; forces rounding to nearest 10. Family `quick_estimate_with_format_constraint`.
- **Q3 — figural sequence / functional pattern.** Figures 1–5 with grisés counts 1, 4, 9, ?, 25 → figure 4 = 16 ; how many for figure 10 (= 100, `n²`). Confirms `functional_pattern` (item 11, previously first-seen 2023 Q7). Actually present since **2015 at least**.
- **Q4 — NEW: mystery number from clues.** *Nombre mystère : entre 28 et 29, 4 chiffres, chiffre des centièmes = 7, chiffre des dixièmes = chiffre des unités.* Answer = `28,87`. Constraint-satisfaction reasoning on decimal numération. Family `mystery_number_clues`.
- **Q5 — NEW: 3-set Venn diagram with people.** Football/Basketball/Tennis sets with 7 prénoms ; complete I/O table and find who plays most sports. Variant of 2017 Q11 `venn_diagram_multiples` but with 3 sets and contextual data. Family `venn_diagram_3sets`.
- **Q6 — NEW: triangular multiples table.** Pascal-like triangle of numbers (1, 2, 4, 3, 16, 5, 25, 18, 42, 16, 45, 30, 100, 11, 77, 24, 48, 120) with regular structure (each row = multiples of row index). Place `20` and `64`, fill the greyed cell. Family `triangular_multiples_table`.
- **Q7 — multi-step problem.** Two classes (15 + 18 élèves), coût livre `5,25€`, vente `12€`, encaissé `168€ + 288€`. Nombre total de livres vendus ?
- **Q8 — NEW: order a set of numbers written in words.** *Ordonne `deux-mille-cinq-cents, deux-mille-cent-cinq, mille-deux-cent-cinq, cinq-mille-cent-deux, mille-cinq-cent-deux`.* Write in chiffres, then order. Family `order_set_from_words` — extends items 6 and 55.
- **Q9 — multiple equivalent expressions on target `1,2`.** `1,2 = 1,2×__`, `12×__`, `120×__`, `6×__`, `0,6×__`, `0,06×__`. Confirms `multiple_equivalent_expressions` (item 34) since 2015 — previously first-seen 2020 Q10.
- **Q10 — filter items by decimal range (multi-select).** Six poulets à peser ; cocher ceux dont le poids est entre `1,3 kg` et `1,4 kg`. Confirms `place_in_interval` (item 22, 2022 Q10) since 2015.
- **Q11 — NEW: number addition pyramid.** Briques pyramide où chaque brique = somme des deux en dessous ; exemple donné avec soustraction inverse. Compléter avec `380, 121, ?, 600` partiellement donnés. Family `number_addition_pyramid`.
- **Q12 — NEW: multi-scale number line.** Une position sur une droite à graduations unitaires correspond à `15` ; à dixième donne trois positions (zoom x10) ; à centième donne trois positions (zoom x100) avec `15,32` et `15,36` données. Cross-zoom visual. Family `multi_scale_number_line` — extends items 25 (number_line_fill) and 32 (number_line_place).
- **Q13 — NEW: operation tree fill.** Arbre binaire d'opérations (`(200+4)×5 = 1 020`) ; compléter un autre arbre `(__+7)×__=4 870`. Each node is an *operation* applied to two inputs. Distinct from `decomposition_tree` (item 5) which is multiplicative-only. Family `operation_tree_fill`.
- **Q14 — NEW: operation tree what-if.** Given arbre `(155+15)×4 = 680`, replace the `4` by `5` — result devient `680+170=850`. MCQ : *le résultat est augmenté de 1 / 5 / 170 / 680*. **Investigation-rich** — tests functional reasoning ("how does the output change when one input changes") — a form of proto-algebraic thinking. Family `operation_tree_what_if`.
- **Q15 — NEW: digit swap effect on sum.** Addition posée `3452 + 6798` ; (a) swap `2` (in `3452`) with `8` (in `6798`) across rows ; (b) swap `5` and `2` within the same row. Le résultat augmente/reste/diminue ? Tests **positional value understanding** — critical misconception probe (many students think "permutation = same result"). Family `digit_swap_effect_on_sum`.
- **Q16 — arbre de décomposition récursif sur `27 000` puis `96 000`.** Same family as item 16 (arbre récursif sur décimal/entier). Confirmed 2015.

## Cross-year signals — important back-dating

2015 is a goldmine: it confirms that several templates previously tagged "first-seen" 2020, 2022, or 2023 were **already present in 2015**:

- `functional_pattern` (item 11) — was tagged 2023 Q7, actually 2015+
- `multiple_equivalent_expressions` (item 34) — was tagged 2020 Q10, actually 2015+
- `place_in_interval` (item 22) — was tagged 2022 Q10, actually 2015+
- `pure_compute_timed_block` (item 30) — was tagged 2020 Q3, actually 2015+ (though 2015's version isn't explicitly timed)
- `digit_permutation_optimize` pattern — was tagged 2016 Q10, but 2015 Q4 (mystery number from clues) is even earlier variant of digit-manipulation reasoning

2015 also introduces the **operation tree** as a distinct primitive — `operation_tree_fill` + `operation_tree_what_if` in sequence (Q13+Q14) suggests the FWB designed them as a 2-step probe: first fluency, then functional reasoning.
