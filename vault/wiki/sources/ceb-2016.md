The **2016 CEB** ([[entities/ceb]]). Math arithmetic booklet = **Livret 8 Nombres et opérations, /50 pts, Mardi 21 juin 2016, 20 questions** (PDF pages 130–150 of `raw/CEB_2016_Questionnaires.pdf`).

## Full question inventory

- **Q1 — 10 ops in 10 min timed block.** `8000−475, 311+195+89+205, 728÷8, 440×0,25, les deux tiers de 630, 7027−198, 28,55+2,99, 5×230×0×12 (absorption par zéro), 37×1,1, 200% de 67`. Includes fractional operators (*les deux tiers de*), percentages >100%, and multiplication-by-zero trap. `pure_compute_timed_block` already present in 2016.
- **Q2 — NEW: common estimation of equivalent ops.** *Toutes ces opérations arrivent au même résultat. `7920÷800`, `1,5×6,6`, `68−58,1`, `3,475+6,425`. Entoure l'estimation la plus proche de ce résultat : `1, 10, 100, 1000`.* Tests transitive estimation — one answer applies to 4 ops because they all equal ~10. New family `common_estimation_of_equivalent_ops`.
- **Q3 — estimation interval.** *`6,8×3,17` se situe : `6<...<10`, `10<...<13`, `13<...<19`, `19<...<23`*. Item 6 variant — *interval* estimation instead of single-value approximation.
- **Q4 — numération en mots → chiffres** (item 9).
- **Q5 — NEW: number grid pattern fill.** Grille 5×5 avec progression par ligne (+0,01) et par colonne (+0,10) ; quelques cellules données, compléter deux cases en gras. Additive variant of `multiplication_grid_fragment` (2022 Q24). Family `number_grid_pattern_fill`.
- **Q6 — place virgule** (item 9).
- **Q7 — NEW: number reading MCQ.** *Comment ce nombre se lit-il ? `187 536` → cent-dix-huit-mille..., cent-quatre-vingt-sept-mille..., dix-huit-mille..., cent-quatre-vingt-mille-sept-cent-trente-six*. Inverse of item 9 (écriture-en-chiffres). Family `number_reading_mcq`.
- **Q8 — multi-row progression grid.** Five rows with independent arithmetic progressions, some cells in bold missing. Extends Q5 but with different progressions per row — tests recognition of multiple concurrent patterns. Variant of `number_grid_pattern_fill`.
- **Q9 — NEW: receipt multi-query.** *Observe ce ticket de caisse. (a) Article le moins cher ? (b) Le plus cher ? (c) Le plus proche de `10€` ? (d) Articles < `1€` ?* Four sub-items on one contextual data source. Family `receipt_multi_query` — realistic data-literacy item.
- **Q10 — NEW: digit permutation optimize.** *Voici `1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6`. (a) Écris le plus grand nombre entier en les utilisant une seule fois chacun. (b) Écris l'entier le plus proche et différent de `341 526`.* Extends `delete_digit_optimize` (2022 Q4) / `digit_punctuation_optimize` (2019 Q20). New family `digit_permutation_optimize`.
- **Q11 — multiple equivalent expressions on target 1.** `1 = 0,675+__ ; 1 = 0,50×__ ; 1 = 0,951+__ ; 1 = 8×__ ; 1 = 1/2 de __`. Same family as 2020 Q10 `multiple_equivalent_expressions`, with target = 1 (complément à 1). Confirms family pre-existed 2020 by at least 4 years.
- **Q12 — NEW: LCM common stops on a game board.** *Plateau de 80 cases. Marita avance de 6, Tony de 8. (a) Première case commune ? (b) Autres cases communes ?* Mathematical game-board puzzle — PPCM (`LCM(6,8)=24`) in disguise. Combines [[concepts/tables-multiplication]] with common-multiples reasoning. Family `lcm_common_stops`.
- **Q13 — abaque read + add chip.** Classic item 14 (`abaque_read`) plus add one jeton in the centaines column.
- **Q14 — NEW: partial product explain (MCQ).** *Multiplication écrite `389 × 23`. Les 778 dizaines correspondent à…* options : `20×389 ; 3×389 ; (2×9)+(2×80)+(2×300) ; (20×9)+(20×80)+(20×300) ; (2×389)`. Multi-correct. MCQ sister of 2020 Q8 `long_multiplication_partial_product_read`. Family `multiplication_partial_product_explain`.
- **Q15 — comparaison fraction/décimal/mots** (item 7). Five sub-items.
- **Q16 — pick correct decompositions of `660÷15`.** Multi-select. Same family as 2017 `pick_equivalents` (item 1) but for division.
- **Q17 — pick correct decompositions of `12×25`.** Multi-select. Classic item 1.
- **Q18 — complète les égalités.** Five sub-items — compensation/propriétés : `76,4+83,9 = __+84`, `792÷8 = (800÷8)−(__÷8)`, `4×7×25 = __×100`, `128÷16 = 64÷__`, `805−__ = 800−242`. Item 2 (`operator_identity_fill`), one of the richest variants seen.
- **Q19 — match situation → operation (6 options).** Same family as 2022 Q14, 2019 Q14. Four situations, six operation cards (A–F) to assign. `match_situation_to_operation`. Confirms continuity pre-2019.
- **Q20 — multi-step logic problem.** École à 2 classes, 28 garçons, classe 1 = 14 filles + 13 garçons, classe 2 = 26 élèves. Filles en classe 2 ? Tests implicit constraint reasoning.

## Cross-year signals

- **`pure_compute_timed_block` confirmed 2016+** (2017 and 2018 match structure exactly). Same stopwatch-image cover page.
- **`match_situation_to_operation` confirmed 2016+** — previously first-seen 2022 Q14. Template existed at least 6 years earlier. Correction to the catalog's "first-seen" tags.
- **`multiple_equivalent_expressions` confirmed 2016+** (Q11) — also older than previously tagged.
- **Fractional operator vocabulary** (*deux tiers de*) already tested in Q1 — pre-dates 2020 Q19 `operator_vocabulary_fill` as pure-compute item.
- **Percentages** (200% in Q1) confirmed as standard Champ-3 calc since 2016, consistent with 2018 (60%), 2017 (75%), 2020 (60%).
