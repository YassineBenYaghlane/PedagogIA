The **2017 CEB** ([[entities/ceb]]). Math arithmetic booklet = **Livret 8 Nombres et opérations, /70 pts, Mardi 20 juin 2017, 22 questions** (PDF pages 113–131 of `raw/CEB_2017_Questionnaires.pdf`).

## Full question inventory

- **Q1 — timed pure-compute, 10 ops in 10 min.** `639÷9, 19,98+7,52, 16×2,3×0, __+17,5=38,6, 75% de 44000, __×1,2=8,4, 200000−480, 2250+330+1750+__=5000, 888×0,125, 5032−298`. Identical structure to 2018 Q1. Confirms `pure_compute_timed_block` existed since at least 2017.
- **Q2 — estimation MCQ** (4 ops).
- **Q3 — reverse estimation.** *NEW.* Given a target (500 or 4), pick the operation whose result is closest. Complementary to standard estimation (item 6).
- **Q4 — vérification d'une division posée** (item 3).
- **Q5 — numération en mots → chiffres** (item 9).
- **Q6 — place virgule** (item 9).
- **Q7 — comptage / suite** décimaux (item 4).
- **Q8 — droite graduée, placement** (item 25 from 2022 Q20). Confirmed.
- **Q9 — arbre de décomposition récursif sur `0,75`** (item 16, same family as 2023 Q23, 2022 Q15).
- **Q10 — équivalence inverse (intrus).** *NEW.* `12/10, 1,02, 1,2, 1200/1000, 6/5, 1,200, 1/2` — barre les **deux** intrus (écritures **non-équivalentes** au même nombre). Negative variant of item 7 — much harder cognitively (identify *which two* of seven don't belong).
- **Q11 — Venn diagram of multiples.** *NEW.* Multiples de 5 × multiples de 8 avec overlap, placer `72, 123, 340, 720` dans le bon ensemble. Visual variant of item 8 (`multiples_diviseurs`) using set-intersection logic.
- **Q12 — Parcours de multiples.** *NEW.* Maze from ligne 1 à ligne 7 ; toutes cases visitées doivent être multiples d'un même nombre (autre que 1). Game-like. Trains flexibility ("quel multiple unifie un chemin possible ?").
- **Q13 — division scale inference.** *NEW.* Given `7 680 ÷ 16 = 480` posée, déduire *sans effectuer* : `7 680 ÷ 16 (avec reste ?)`, `7 685 ÷ 16`, `768 ÷ 16`, `7 680 ÷ 160`, `7 680 ÷ 48`. Tests scale-law for division. Sister to 2023 Q22 `inference_from_known` but for division.
- **Q14 — inference from known product** (item 10). `314 × 52 = 16 328` → `31,4 × 52`, `314 × 0,52`.
- **Q15 — `=` ou `≠` distributivité.** Four sub-items on `43 × 15`: `=43×10×5` (vrai), `=(43×10)+(43×5)` (vrai), `=43×5×3` (vrai), `=(43+10)+(43+5)` (faux). Confirms item family 17 (piège égalité) with distributivité focus.
- **Q16 — signes manquants dans équation** : `2,5 × 72 = (10 [?] 72) [?] 4` ; `874 − 9,7 = (874 [?] 10) [?] 0,3`. Extends `operator_identity_fill` (item 2) — instead of filling in a number, the student fills in the operator symbol.
- **Q17 — calcul dénombrement géometric** (points cachés par nuage). Same as 2022 Q21, 2019 Q8.
- **Q18 — énoncé de problème from operation.** *NEW.* Given `17 × 25 = 425`, write a word problem that matches. **Reverse modeling.** Complements `match_situation_to_operation` (2022 Q14) from the opposite direction.
- **Q19 — soustraction posée avec chiffres manquants.** *NEW.* `73_ _ − 5_71 = _281`. Classic algorithm reconstruction: student must reason backward through the subtraction algorithm.
- **Q20 — multi-résolution picker** (item 18, same as 2024 L7 Q13). 4 résolutions A/B/C/D, one correct.
- **Q21 — problème pourcentage parking.** Multi-step: 6 niveaux (2×100 + 4×80 = 520 places), 60% occupées, combien libres ?
- **Q22 — countdown target.** *NEW.* *Tu dois obtenir `730` au départ des nombres `4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 25`. Utilise-les une seule fois chacun, pas nécessairement tous.* Countdown-style combinatorial puzzle. Extremely rich — tests flexibility of arithmetic operations, not just accuracy.

Eight new families not previously in the catalog:

| 48 | `multiples_path_maze` | Q12 | game-like |
| 49 | `division_scale_inference` | Q13 | div sister of #10 |

| 51 | `posed_operation_missing_digits` | Q19 | algorithm reconstruction |
| 52 | `countdown_target` | Q22 | combinatorial puzzle |

2017 also reuses `operator_sign_fill` (Q16) — which is a variant of item 2 (`operator_identity_fill`) that slots operators instead of numbers; worth treating as a distinct shape. Adding as **#53 `operator_sign_fill`** to the catalog.

## Cross-year signals

- **Pure-compute timed block (`pure_compute_timed_block`)** was already in use in 2017, not introduced in 2018/2020. Shifts the "first-seen" tag for that template.
- **Countdown-style puzzle (Q22)** and **maze (Q12)** are the most *playful* items surveyed so far — they preview the gamification angle PedagogIA is aiming at. Worth noting: FWB was already using these 7 years ago.
- **Problem-write (Q18) and multi-résolution picker (Q20)** both appear in 2017, confirming modeling + reasoning-chain probing existed years before the 2024 incarnation.
