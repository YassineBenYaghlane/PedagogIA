The **2019 CEB** ([[entities/ceb]]). Math arithmetic booklet (Livret Nombres et opérations, ~25 questions, /70 pts). Cataloged by reading PDF pages 35–50 of `raw/CEB_2019_Questionnaires.pdf`.

## What 2019 confirms

The core nine items of [[concepts/ceb-attendus-p6-arithmetique]] all reappear (estimation, propriétés opératoires fill, vérification, suite, arbre, fraction↔décimal↔mots, multiples/diviseurs, numération-rang, pure-compute). 2019 also reuses several 2020 templates (compensation, abaque-style numération). Stable cross-year backbone.

## New item types surfaced by 2019

### Q16 — `midpoint_check`

> *Peut-on placer le nombre `500` juste au milieu de ces segments ? `250 ⊢ 750`, `125 ⊢ 375`, `380 ⊢ 620`.*

Tests notion of *middle / median / average* on a number line — implicit calcul de moyenne arithmétique `(a+b)/2`. Three sub-items, two false. Pure-reasoning probe distinct from `number_line_place` (2020 Q5) and `number_line_fill` (2022 Q20): tests the *property* of midpoint, not its construction.

### Q20 — `digit_punctuation_optimize`

> *PLACE une virgule dans chacun des termes de l'addition pour obtenir `284`. `9664 + 658 + 4836 + 732 = 284`.*

Generalises 2022 Q4 (`delete_digit_optimize`): instead of deleting a zero, the student must **insert** a virgule in each term so that the sum hits a target. Tests place-value reasoning + estimation — multiple solutions exist; only some make the sum work.

### Q25 — `fraction_ordering_strategy_layered`

> *(a) `7/12, 3/12, 8/12, 11/12` (même dénominateur). (b) `5/3, 5/8, 5/6, 5/2` (même numérateur). (c) `1/3, 5/10, 6/5, 12/16` (mixte).*

A single-question template with **three sub-items, each isolating a specific ordering strategy**: same-denominator (compare numerators), same-numerator (compare denominators inversely), mixed (must convert or reason structurally). This is a teaching-aware template — a student who fails (b) but succeeds (a) reveals a clean misconception ("plus grand denominateur = plus grand fraction"). Adds didactic structure on top of `order_set` (item 6 from 2022).

### Q14 — extended `match_situation_to_operation`

> *Pack de 6 bouteilles à 1,5 L. Coche le calcul qui te permet de savoir combien de bouteilles dans 36 packs : `36+6`, `36×1,5`, `36×6`, `36÷1,5`, `6×1,5`, `(36×6)×1,5`.*

Variant of 2022 Q14 with **six candidates** instead of four, including correct multi-step (`(36×6)×1,5` for litres, `36×6` for bottles). Same modeling family but probes deeper distractor space (units mixing, false multiplication chain).

## Items confirmed (2019 reappearance)

- `propriétés_opératoires_fill` (Q9, Q19) — heavy presence
- `inference_from_known` (Q23) — same as 2023 Q22 (`26×48=1248` → infer scaling)
- `multiplication_grid_fill` (Q24) — Pythagore partielle avec décimaux
- `decomposition_tree` (Q17) — addition variant on `10`
- `divisibility_complete_digit` (Q18)
- `multiples_intersection_circle` (Q22) — multiples de 3 ET de 7
