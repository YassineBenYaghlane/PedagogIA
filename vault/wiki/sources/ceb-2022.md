The **2022 edition of the Certificat d'Études de Base** ([[entities/ceb]]). Ingestion scope (per user focus): math booklets only. Located the **Livret Nombres et opérations** at PDF pages ~80–99, holding **26 questions / 17 + ~50 ≈ 70 pts** (single-booklet format like 2023, not the 2024 split). Surrounding pages cover Éveil (histoire/géo, sciences) and other math strands which were scanned but not deeply synthesized.

## What 2022 confirms vs the cross-year synthesis

The core nine item families catalogued in [[concepts/ceb-attendus-p6-arithmetique]] (decomposition, propriétés opératoires fill, vérification, suite, arbre de décomposition, estimation, fraction↔décimal↔mots, multiples/diviseurs, numération-rang) all reappear in 2022 — confirming their *hard-core* status across at least three years (2022/2023/2024).

Belgian numération is again present (Q9: *deux-cent-septante-trois millièmes*).

## New item types surfaced by 2022 (not in 2023 or 2024)

Eight item families add to the catalog. Each is a distinct exercise template for [[concepts/ceb-attendus-p6-arithmetique]]:

### Q4 — `delete_digit_optimize`

> *Voici `105 040,07`. Barre un zéro pour rendre le nombre le plus grand possible. Puis le plus petit possible.*

Tests place-value reasoning by *deletion*, not by reading. Students must mentally simulate the result of deleting each `0` and compare. Powerful misconception probe — many P6 students will treat all zeros as equivalent.

### Q10 — `place_in_interval` (decimal range membership)

> *Parmi `6,66 / 6,06 / 6,6 / 6,006`, entoure celui qui peut se placer entre `6,01` et `6,5`. Puis entre `6` et `6,01`. Puis entre `6,5` et `6,650`.*

Three sub-items reuse the same answer pool — tests fine-grained ordering on décimaux au millième. Distinct from `comparison` (pairwise) and `order_set` (ranking) — this is *interval membership*.

### Q11 — `place_in_interval` (mixed fraction + decimal)

> *`2 < ? < 3` : entoure les deux nombres parmi `2/3, 3/2, 3,2, 2,32, 5/2`.*

Same shape as Q10 but with mixed notations — students who can't convert `5/2` to `2,5` fail. Pairs naturally with the fraction↔décimal equivalence item type.

### Q14 — `match_situation_to_operation` (modeling)

> *Plateau de jeu. Quatre situations (recule de 5 cases depuis la 25ᵉ ; pour arriver sur la 25ᵉ a avancé de 5 ; etc.) à associer aux quatre opérations `?+5=25`, `?−25=5`, `5+25=?`, `25−5=?`.*

Tests **modeling** — choosing the right operation given a situation, *before* computing. This is exactly the layer where the référentiel ([[concepts/resolution-problemes-math]]) names *modéliser* as a distinct compétence. Very high investigation value: a student who picks `25−5=?` for a "recule depuis la 25ᵉ" situation has a clean misconception signature on inverse operation reasoning.

### Q17 — `equivalent_products_grid` (triangle of equivalences)

> *Trois triangles avec un produit central et trois facteurs périphériques à compléter. Ex: triangle central `720` avec `9 × 80`, `90 × _`, `0,9 × _`. Triangle central `0,24` avec `0,6 × 0,4`, `6 × _`, `0,06 × _`.*

Tests scale invariance of multiplication (×10ⁿ with n positive *and* negative). Generalizes 2024 L6 Q12 (the linear `× 0,07 / 0,7 / 7 / 70 / 700` grid) into a multi-anchor visual with smaller-product start points.

### Q20 — `number_line_fill`

> *Complète les cases blanches : `_ _ 18,01 18,02 18,03` ; `_ 2 000 2 125 _ 2 375`.*

Number-line completion with both decimals (millièmes step) and integers (variable step `+125`). Tests step-size inference + extension. Distinct from `fill_blank sequence` (which our P5 templates have) because the *spacing* is given as graphical positions, not as `+step`.

### Q22 — `division_equivalence`

> *Division `13 698 ÷ 4,5`. Pour effectuer cette division, on transforme le diviseur en `45`. Écris le dividende afin que les quotients soient équivalents.*

Tests the *property* of division: multiplying both terms by 10 preserves the quotient. Conceptual probe — no calcul, just the rule. Closes a hole: PedagogIA tests the *trick* (×0,1 ↔ ×10) but not the *invariance principle*.

### Q24 — `multiplication_grid_fragment`

> *Tableau triangulaire de multiples partiel : `30 33 / 36 _ 44 / 40 _ 50 _ / _ _ 54 _` etc. Complète les trois cases grisées. Puis place `72` dans les deux cases qui conviennent.*

A *fragment* of a multiplication table where students must reconstruct the row/column logic from sparse anchors. Tests multi-table fluency simultaneously (T2, T6, T9, T10, T11, T12 visible). Good probe of [[concepts/tables-multiplication]] mastery.

### Q26 — `inverse_operation_consistency`

> *Sous trois taches se trouve le même nombre. Coche la seule opération où la tache recouvre un nombre différent : `4000 − 152 = X`, `4000 − X = 152`, `X − 152 = 4000`, `X + 152 = 4000`.*

Tests fluency with inverse-operation rewrites. Three forms have `X = 3848`; the trap is `X − 152 = 4000` → `X = 4152`. Same family as `verification_pick` (item 3) but staged as consistency-check across four equivalent forms. Excellent for [[concepts/egalite-mathematique]] probing.

## Other notable items

- **Q3a — pure-compute block (9 operations).** Confirms the 2023 pattern: P6 still gets a direct-algorithm fluency probe. Includes `5 × 5 × 5` (chained mult — relates to 2024's "factorielle" item) and `(51 × 100) + (23 × 0,1)` (mixed-magnitude composition).
- **Q6 — multi-classification of a sequence.** *0, 12, 24, 36, 48, 60, 72, 84 sont… diviseurs de 12 / multiples de 12 / premiers / impairs / pairs / entiers.* Three correct (multiples, pairs, entiers). Variant of multiples/diviseurs item — adds *pairs*/*premiers*/*entiers* attributes; tests vocabulary overload, classic P6 confusion site.
- **Q15 — arbre de décomposition récursif sur 42 (4-level binary tree).** Same family as 2023 Q23 (`4,8`) — confirms this item rotates.
- **Q18 — problème convives (220 personnes, 12 par rect, 6 par rond, ? par ovale).** Multi-step + visual table layout; tests subtraction of partial counts then division.
- **Q21 — dénombrement avec motif caché derrière miroir.** Multiplication géometrique avec inférence (compte les hiboux y compris cachés). Hybrid math/spatial reasoning — borderline arithmétique/géometrie.
- **Q25 — menu calorique.** Sum of values from a table + cross-comparison (`1 sachet chips = combien de pommes ?`). Heavy [[concepts/proportionnalite-directe]] flavor.

## Why this matters for PedagogIA

- **Modeling probe.** Q14 is the first item in the catalog that explicitly separates *modeling* from *computing*. The référentiel names this as a distinct compétence; until 2022, the only proxy in the CEB was the multi-résolution picker (2024 L7 Q13). Two distinct shapes for the same compétence → both worth implementing.
- **Number line as a primitive.** Q20 promotes the number line from "support visuel" (already used in décimaux comparison) to *answer surface*. Adds a UI primitive but unlocks several more items down the line.
- **Trois ans suffisent à séparer "core" et "rotating".** Items 1, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9 = présents 3/3 années. Items 2, 3, 12, 15 = 2/3. Le reste = 1/3. Cette stabilité doit informer les **mastery thresholds** : strict pour le core (3/3), tolérant pour le rotating (1/3).
