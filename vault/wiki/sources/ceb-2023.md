The **2023 edition of the Certificat d'Études de Base** ([[entities/ceb]]) — 156-page FWB booklet covering the usual Français / Éveil / Math strands, administered in late June 2023. Ingested scope (per user focus): the arithmetic booklet only. See [[sources/ceb-2024]] for the companion year and [[concepts/ceb-attendus-p6-arithmetique]] for the cross-year item synthesis.

## Structural difference vs 2024

The 2023 CEB bundles *Nombres et opérations* into a **single Livret 8 (/70 pts)** instead of 2024's split (Livret 6 + 7, /36 + /34 = /70). Point budget is identical; the reorganization is purely logistical. Worth flagging: if we mine past CEBs programmatically by booklet filename, we can't assume a fixed livret numbering — 2023's "Livret 8" and 2024's "Livret 6+7" cover the same domain.

| Year | Arithmetic livret(s) | Points | Questions | Exam date |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2023 | Livret 8 — Nombres et opérations | /70 | 25 | Ven 30/06/2023 |
| 2024 | Livret 6 — Nombres et opérations 1 | /36 | 15 | Jeu 27/06/2024 |
| 2024 | Livret 7 — Nombres et opérations 2 | /34 | 15 | Jeu 27/06/2024 |

Livrets 3 (Solides et figures) and 4 (Grandeurs) are also present in 2023 but out of this ingest's scope.

## 2023 arithmetic items (Livret 8)

The 25 items span the same nine core types identified in [[concepts/ceb-attendus-p6-arithmetique]], plus four types that appear in 2023 but **not** in 2024. Notable items:

- **Q1 — Estimation.** Four subitems, explicit "sans effectuer l'opération" framing: `13,3 + 103,5 + 32,5`, `1 003,6 − 257,1`, `703 × 19,7`, `203 : 1,9`. In 2024, estimation was one subitem (L6 Q7). **2023 weights this 4× more heavily.**
- **Q3 — Poser 8 opérations.** Direct pure-calcul on both entiers and décimaux: `765+162`, `8 020−40,8`, `0,8+0,55`, `12,3−2,31`, `60×9`, `7×0,8`, `600×0,7`, `7,2:9`. 2024 has **no direct computation-only block** — 2023 tests raw algorithm fluency more directly.
- **Q7 — Suite figurale (relation fonctionnelle).** 1 table → 4 chaises; 2 tables → 6; 3 tables → 8. Compute chaises for 7 tables; then *inverse* — tables for 20 chaises. This is the `y = 2n + 2` precursor to algebra, absent from 2024. Direct signal for a pattern-recognition template.
- **Q8 — Fractions de quantité.** Transfer `¼ de 32 Go + ½ de 64 Go` onto a 250 Go disk, reste? Mixes fraction-operator and subtraction with units. 2024 has no analog; worth a dedicated template.
- **Q9 — Ordre croissant mixte.** Rank `½ | 0,6 | 5/3 | 1/5 | 1`. Mixing proper, improper, unit and non-unit fractions with a decimal — a pure [[concepts/fractions-partage-vs-nombre]] stress test.
- **Q11 — Abaque formel.** Reads three numbers from a `CM DM UM C D U | d c` column table; compare, extract "centaines entières dans 3 692,74". 2024 does rank reasoning (L7 Q9) but not via the formal abaque notation — a distinct input primitive worth implementing.
- **Q13 — Properties 2D grid.** Cross-tabulates a number against *impair / multiple de 36 / diviseur de 36 / double de 36*, then against *pair / tiers de 99 / multiple de 99 / diviseur de 99 / premier*. Nombres premiers appear explicitly — a concept the référentiel lists P6 but 2024 doesn't test.
- **Q14 — Compléments à X.** Three tableaux at scales 1 000 / 100 / 1: `730 + _ = 1 000`, `51,9 + _ = 100`, `0,38 + _ = 1`. Tests scale invariance of compensation.
- **Q17 — `100 − 12,5 ○ 12,5 − 100`.** A trap item: students often complete with `=` by missing the non-commutativity of subtraction. Direct probe of [[concepts/egalite-mathematique]] and a classic prerequisite misconception.
- **Q22 — Multiplicative inference.** *Si `21 × 43 = 903` alors* `2,1 × 43 = ?`, `42 × 43 = ?`, `21 × 44 = ?`, `22 × 43 = ?`. Derive three results from a single known product using decimal scaling, doubling, and distributive additions. Beautifully compact template — a single-prompt mastery probe for P6 multiplicative reasoning.
- **Q23 — Arbre de décomposition récursif.** Decompose `4,8` into *parts équivalentes* through a **3-level binary tree** (two halves → three parts → three doubles). Much more complex than 2024's `15 000` arbre (L6 Q6). Tests multiplicative structure on a decimal.
- **Q25 — Décomposition distributive.** `56 × 25 = (50 × 20)+(6 × 5)` · `(56 × 20)+(56 × 5)` · `(56 × 20)+5` · `(56 × 100):4` · `(56 + 20) × 5`. Two correct answers (distributivity vs division-by-4 trick). Same pattern as 2024 L6 Q3 — this item type is **highly stable** across years.

## Year-over-year stability (what repeats 2023 → 2024)

High-frequency cross-year items:

- Estimation / ordre de grandeur
- Décomposition multiplicative (distributivité)
- Multiples / diviseurs / critères de divisibilité
- Droite graduée (placement et lecture)
- Opérations sur décimaux au millième
- Opérations réciproques / vérification
- Propriétés opératoires comme fill-in
- Problèmes contextualisés multi-étape
- Numération belge (*septante*, *nonante*, *quatre-vingts*) — confirmed in 2023 Q10 (*seize-mille-neuf-cent-**nonante**-huit*)

2023-only (not in 2024 arithmetic):

- Pure-compute block (Q3 direct algorithm fluency)
- Suite figurale / relation fonctionnelle (Q7)
- Fractions de quantité (Q8)
- Ordre croissant mixte fractions + décimaux (Q9)
- Abaque formel comme notation de lecture (Q11)
- Nombres premiers (Q13)
- Arbre de décomposition récursif sur décimal (Q23)

2024-only (not in 2023 arithmetic):

- Multi-résolution problem picker (4 chaînes candidates — L7 Q13)
- Table multiplicative vide `× 0,07 / 0,7 / 7 / 70 / 700` (L6 Q12)
- Multiplication factorielle `12 × 8 = 12 × 2 × 2 × 2` (L6 Q13)

## Why this matters for PedagogIA

- **Two years doubles the template yield.** 2023 adds at minimum seven distinct item types not present in 2024 — all well-specified, all P6-calibrated. If we mine both years, we get ≈16 reusable arithmetic item families.
- **Stability signal.** Cross-year repetition tells us which attendus are *hard core* (never skipped) vs *rotated*. Mastery thresholds for the hard core should be stricter.
- **Template families to prioritize from 2023:** Q7 (suite figurale), Q8 (fraction-de-quantité), Q22 (inference from known product), Q23 (arbre récursif sur décimal). None of these are currently in our P6 template set.
- **The `100 − 12,5 vs 12,5 − 100` trap (Q17)** is a canonical investigation probe: a student who marks `=` gives us a direct misconception signature on commutativité de la soustraction.
