The **2024 edition of the Certificat d'Études de Base** — the annual FWB external exam every P6 student sits to certify end-of-primary achievement. Full questionnaire booklet (152 pages, Juin 2024), covering Français (Écouter, Lire, Écrire), Éveil (Histoire/Géo, Sciences), and the three math strands (Solides et figures, Grandeurs, Nombres et opérations). Distributed over five exam days (24–28 juin 2024). See [[entities/ceb]] for the exam itself.

Total math = **165 points across 4 booklets**; arithmetic alone = **70 points across 2 booklets** (Nombres et opérations 1 + 2). The other math booklets (Grandeurs, Solides et figures) scan but aren't synthesized here — focus of this ingest is arithmetic.

## Key arithmetic takeaways (Livrets 6 + 7)

- **Décomposition as a first-class skill.** Multiple questions (L6 Q3, Q8, Q10, Q12; L7 Q8) ask students to recognize *equivalent* rewrites: `15 × 45 = (10 × 45) + (5 × 45)`, `12,5 × 48 = (10 × 48) + (2,5 × 48)`, `12 × 8 = 12 × 2 × 2 × 2`. This validates the multi-strategy explanation model in PedagogIA — an answer can be wrong on the final number but right on the decomposition (or vice versa).
- **Vérification is examined.** L6 Q4 explicitly asks *which operation verifies `886 432 : 4 = 221 608`* — `221 608 × 4`. The référentiel names vérification as an attendu from P3 onward ([[concepts/resolution-problemes-math]]); the CEB confirms it is an independently-scored item.
- **Multi-résolution problem item (L7 Q13).** Four candidate resolution chains are shown; the student picks which arithmetic reasoning is valid. Three of the four encode classic P6 misconceptions (double subtraction, wrong dividend, wrong grouping). This is exactly the investigation signal we want Claude to produce. See [[concepts/resolution-problemes-math]] for the expanded example.
- **Numération up to millions, décimaux to millièmes.** L7 Q7 writes *Sept millions quatre-vingt-mille-vingt-quatre* and *Cinq-mille-septante-trois unités douze millièmes*; L7 Q5 places the virgule at different ranks in `245681`; L7 Q9 identifies the rang of a digit in `721 546,03`. Matches the P6 extension of [[concepts/numeration-decimale]] (milliards in référentiel, but exam caps at millions + millièmes).
- **Fraction-décimal equivalence.** L6 Q9 and L7 Q8 demand fluency between notations: `25/10 = 2,5`, `14,65 = 1 465/100`, `75/100 = trois quarts ≠ 0,075`. This is the hinge where [[concepts/fractions-partage-vs-nombre]] flips into Champ 3 territory.
- **Multiples / diviseurs / critères.** L7 Q2 ("multiples de 8 entre 1 et 37", "au moins un nombre impair diviseur de 18") and L7 Q14 (complete a digit so `237_` is multiple of 2 / 5 / neither) — baseline P5-P6 items, useful canonical probes.
- **Suites arithmétiques as distinct item (L6 Q5).** `840 + 70 + 70 + 70 …`, `1 080 + 90 + 90 + 90 …`. Cheap to parameterize in our templates; graded independently of operation mastery.
- **Propriétés opératoires as fill-in.** L6 Q8: `9 × 67,2 = (_ × 67,2) − 67,2`, `760 : 0,1 = 760 × _`, `6 498 − 379 = 6 500 − _`. Tests distributivity, inverse relation `÷0,1 ↔ ×10`, compensation in soustraction. Currently thin in our template set — worth adding.
- **Real-world problem contexts.** Train schedules (L4 Q18), jus de fruits for anniversaire (L7 Q6), pralines by flavor (L7 Q12), paquets de feuilles across classes (L7 Q13), pommeau de douche économique (L4 Q5), jar of bonbons → kg (L4 Q10). The *situation contextualisée* framing the référentiel mandates ([[concepts/resolution-problemes-math]]) is what the CEB actually grades.

## Distinctively Belgian numération (worth noting for the UI)

L7 Q7 uses **septante** (70) and **quatre-vingts** (80); L7 Q8 references *septante-cinq dixièmes*. The référentiel and CEB both normalize Belgian French number words — `septante`, `nonante`, but **`quatre-vingts`** (not *octante*). Our number-word → digits parser must accept both Belgian and French variants. Flag for the frontend.

## Out of POC but indexed

- **Livret 3 (Solides et figures)** — polygones, symétrie axiale, solides polyèdres/non polyèdres, construction par programme. Matches [[concepts/champ-1-geometrie]]; thin in our wiki, out of POC.
- **Livret 4 (Grandeurs)** — litres/mètres/grammes préfixes, aire, périmètre, volume, calendrier, durée, pourcentages (L4 Q16: 87 % de 2 500), tableau de proportionnalité (L4 Q15). The pourcentages + proportionnalité items **overlap with Champ 3** and could feed POC templates if we widen scope. See [[concepts/champ-2-grandeurs]], [[concepts/proportionnalite-directe]].
- **Livrets 1, 5, 10** — Français écouter, histoire-géo, sciences. Out of scope.

## Why this matters for PedagogIA

- The CEB is **ground truth for P6 exit-level competence in the FWB**. The référentiel says what should be taught; the CEB shows what is actually tested. Every template we ship for P6 should have an analog item in this exam.
- The distribution of points across the three math strands (40/55/70) tells us how the FWB *weighs* them. Arithmetic is biggest — validates POC scope.
- Several items (L6 Q3, L7 Q13, L6 Q8) are **almost drop-in prompts** for exercise templates. Worth mining when we expand the P6 template set.
